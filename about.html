Est. 1970s

The Legacy of the Lake

The Founder: Mary Auma Oliech, known as Mama Oliech, was the mother of legendary Kenyan footballer Dennis Oliech. She established the business to provide for her children.Global Spotlight: The restaurant gained international fame when Facebook CEO Mark Zuckerberg visited in September 2016, putting traditional Kenyan food on the global map.Tragic Loss: The family matriarch passed away after a long battle with cancer in July 2018.Current Operations: Following her passing, the restaurant has continued to be run by the Oliech family, specifically her daughter Lilian Oliech.Why It's FamousThe restaurant is celebrated for authentic, traditional Kenyan dishes, particularly its signature whole deep-fried lake tilapia served with wet fry, millet or cornmeal ugali, and fresh local vegetables.It has become a staple "must-visit" dining spot in the city for locals, government officials, and international figures alike, including distinguished guests like former US President Barack Obama and actor Idris Elba.

Roots from the Lake

Our story is inextricably linked to the waters of Lake Victoria. Every morning, before the city stirs, our trusted network of fishermen harvests the finest Tilapia. This isn't just sourcing; it's a sacred trust passed down through generations.

"The taste of the lake must reach the plate as if the water is still whispering to the fish."

Legendary Guests

Barack Obama

The 44th US President’s visit cemented our status as a global landmark. He specifically requested our signature whole-fried tilapia, bringing worldwide attention to Kenyan culinary heritage.

Mark Zuckerberg

The Facebook founder famously dined with us during his first trip to Africa, praising the authenticity of the experience and introducing Mama Oliech to the global tech community.

Idris Elba

A regular when visiting the region, the actor has often cited Mama Oliech as his favorite haunt for 'soul food' in Nairobi, appreciating the unpretentious but world-class flavor.
